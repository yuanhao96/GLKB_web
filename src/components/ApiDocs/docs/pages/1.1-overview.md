## Beyond manual review

A researcher investigating 1,500 candidate genes cannot read 15,000 abstracts — but automating with a general LLM trades reliability for speed. GLKB addresses both: send a list of genes, variants, or drug targets and get back cited answers, grounded in 263M+ biomedical terms and live PubMed.

---

## Why GLKB

- **Scale** — Run hundreds of genes, variants, or drug targets in parallel. 1,500 genes complete in ~30 minutes.
- **Reliability** — Every claim is linked to a PubMed ID. Gaps are labeled "none found" and never fabricated.
- **Structured output** — Define a schema in your prompt. Empty fields mean no evidence, not a guess.
- **Reproducibility** — The same prompt on the same list produces consistent, parseable output every time.

## What you can build

| Workflow | What you send | What you get back |
|---|---|---|
| Gene prioritization | 1,500 candidate genes from a screen or GWAS | Literature score + evidence + PMIDs per gene |
| DEG annotation | 800 differentially expressed genes from RNA-seq | Function, disease association, stress direction per gene |
| Drug target dossiers | 50 candidate kinase targets | Mechanism, human genetics, known modulators, clinical stage |
| GWAS locus interpretation | 200 fine-mapped locus genes | Functional evidence, model organism phenotype, priority tier |
| Variant interpretation | Gene:variant strings from exome sequencing | Domain context, pathogenic variant literature, ClinVar context |
| Proteomics annotation | 400 proteins from a mass-spec experiment | Known function, interaction partners, perturbation evidence |

## Knowledge base

Built from the full PubMed corpus — **263 million biomedical terms**, **14.6 million relationships**, encoding gene–disease associations, pathway memberships, and ontology hierarchies.
